Why DIDs are Important

DIDs enable user-controlled, verifiable digital identifiers that reduce reliance on centralized registries or identity providers. They improve privacy, portability, and interoperability for digital identity systems.

Key benefits (design goals)

  • Decentralization: No single authority controls the identifier.
  • Control: Controllers can manage their own identifiers and keys.
  • Privacy: Minimal disclosure and selective sharing are possible.
  • Interoperability & Portability: Work across systems and networks.

Real-world importance

DIDs support verifiable credentials, secure agent-to-agent communication, and use-cases that require strong authentication without centralized identity providers. Examples include education credentials, IoT device identities, and human-centric identity wallets.
